Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

This is my family's favorite cookie recipe. They are not too crunchy, not too chewy. They also have a great balance in flavor between the rather cakey cookie portion and the milk chocolate chips. My mother used to bake them occasionally when I was a child. I learned to bake as a teen, and baked them quite frequently. I look forward to helping my own children bake them someday.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup vegetable shortening
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla
  • 1 12oz package of milk chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a medium sized bowl, stir together all the dry ingredients, except the chocolate chips. Set aside.
  3. In a separate large sized bowl, beat together the butter and shortening with an electric mixer.
  4. Add the sugars to the butter mixture and beat until fluffy.
  5. Add the eggs and vanilla to the wet mixture and beat well.
  6. Gradually add the dry mixture to the wet mixture, beating until well combined.
  7. Stir in chocolate chips.
  8. Drop batter in tablespoonfuls about 2 inches apart on an ungreased cookie sheet.
  9. Bake for 8 to ten minutes.
  10. Remove from oven and cool.
